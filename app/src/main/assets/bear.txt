Habitat
North American brown bears can be found in many habitats, from the fringes of deserts to high mountain forests and ice fields (though, they generally prefer forests).
Polar bears' preferred habitat is, of course, on top of the ice that covers Arctic seas much of the year.
#####
Diet
Bears are generally omnivorous, but dietary preferences range.
Except for the carnivorous polar bear and the vegetarian giant panda, bears consume ants, bees, seeds of trees, roots, nuts, berries, insect larvae such as grubs.
Prey taken by bears include rodents, fish, deer, pigs, and seals.
#####
Physical Characteristics
Bears have large bodies with shaggy hair.
They have powerful, stocky legs that make them very fast runners, while their clawed paws are useful for hunting and digging.
They are adept at climbing and swimming, have a long snout and a short tail.
#####
Known For
Bears are known for their coat of fur and size.
