Habitat
They are found most often in savannas, grasslands, and forests.
However, they do occupy a wide range of habitats, including deserts, swamps, and highlands in tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.
#####
Diet
All elephant species are herbivores, feeding on grasses and leaves and using their powerful trunks to dig for roots or break off large tree branches.
They also eat tree produce (fruits) and tree matter (roots, flowers, twigs, bark, etc.)
Elephants need to consume a large amount of food each day, so they are usually moving to find their next meal.
#####
Physical Characteristics
Elephants are grayish to brown in colour, and their body hair is sparse and coarse.
They have four legs, a tail, and tusks of ivory. Their ears are wide and flat and their long nose (called trunks) that can be about 6-8 feet long.
#####
Known For
The largest living land mammal, known for their tusks and trunks (and the trumpet sounds they make with them).
#####
IUCN Category
The African Savannah Elephant and the Asian Elephant are EN - Endangered. And the African Forest Elephant is CR - Critically Endangered.
